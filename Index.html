2nd Int. Summer School
“PROTEOMICS – from Introduction to Clinical Applications”

Iasi, Romania
July 9th-13th 2018



2nd Int. Summer School “PROTEOMICS – from Introduction to Clinical Applications” will be held at Alexandru Ioan Cuza from Iasi and TRANSCEND - Regional Institute for Oncology, Iasi.

Proteomics is a modern discipline concerned with applying different techniques of molecular biology, biochemistry, and genetics to elucidate the structure, function, and interactions of the proteins produced by the genes of a particular cell, tissue, or organism. In recent years, several mass spectrometric (MS) based technologies were developed for studying proteins and peptides, enzymes and antibodies and their involvement in pathological processes.

The summer school will include three intensive teaching days and one day of practical part. The scientific program will provide an intensive introduction in Proteomics and MS technologies and will discuss also specific MS based methods such as affinity – mass spectrometry and affinity – proteomics; MALDI – Imaging and targeted quantitative proteomics including MRM, and immuno-MALDI. Beside MS approaches an introductory part in next generation sequencing and cell culture approaches will be provided.



Program

The summer school starts on the Sunday and finishes on the following Saturday.

Didactic lectures

The following didactic lectures will be presented during this years summer school:

  • Introduction to biological molecules – structure/function review of: lipids, glycol-lipids, carbohydrates, peptides/proteins, DNA/RNA
  • Fundamentals of mass spectra: base peak, resolution calculations, ppm calculations etc.
  • Ionisation fundamentals (MALDI and ESI)
  • Mass analysers: quads, traps, TOFs, ICRs, Orbitraps, combos
  • Recent developments in Orbitraps
  • Separation strategies: LC, CE, HILIC, fractionation (e.g. SDS-PAGE and TLC)
  • Fragmentation mechanisms: high and low energy CID, ECD/ETD, IRMPD
  • Interpretation of peptide tandem mass spectra including basics of database search
  • Quantitative analysis – relative and absolute

This didactic program will commence on the Monday. Please note that a written examination is distributed on Monday that is collected at week’s end. For those students who pass the examination, credits may be available at their EU home institutions. In some cases the same may be true for Russian institutions, but in these cases the students will have to explore this on their own as we have an agreement only within the EU.

With regard to credits that may be earned in total, the Program will be about 40-45 working hours. Certificates of participation will be provided that will list the actual number of the working hours, with the Exam outcome (pass/fail) which should allow participants to obtain credit in their MSc and PhD programs equal to a given number of credits, including ECTS credits. For instance, if 1 ECTS credit = 25 working hours in a given university, then a participant will be eligible for about 1.6-1.8 ECTS credits.
